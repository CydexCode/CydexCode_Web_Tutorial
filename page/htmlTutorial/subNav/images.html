In HTML, text is encapsulated within the <p> (paragraph) tag, providing a structured way to present paragraphs of information. Headings are defined using <h1> to <h6> tags, with <h1> being the largest and most important heading, and <h6> being the smallest. Line breaks within a paragraph can be achieved using the <br> tag.

Examples:

Paragraphs:

            <p>This is a paragraph of text.</p>
<p>Another paragraph goes here.</p>

Headings:

            <h1>Heading 1</h1>
<h2>Heading 2</h2>
<h3>Heading 3</h3>
<h4>Heading 4</h4>
<h5>Heading 5</h5>
<h6>Heading 6</h6>

Line Breaks:

            <p>This is a line of text.<br>This is a new line of text.</p>

Lists:

Unordered List:

            <ul>
<li>Item 1</li>
<li>Item 2</li>
<li>Item 3</li>
</ul>

Ordered List:

            <ol>
<li>First Item</li>
<li>Second Item</li>
<li>Third Item</li>
</ol>

Links:

Anchor Tag:

            <a href="https://www.example.com">Visit Example.com</a>

Link with Image:

            <a href="https://www.example.com">
<img src="example.jpg" alt="Example Image">
</a>

Images:

Image Tag:

            <img src="example.jpg" alt="Example Image">

Image with Width and Height:

            <img src="example.jpg" alt="Example Image" width="300" height="200">
